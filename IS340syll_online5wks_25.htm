1. Course Objectives: The course introduces students to the fundamentals of Management Information Systems and the relationship of information systems and business organizations. Students will learn the strategic and administrative roles of information systems in business, how information systems enable businesses to gain a competitive advantage, and the role of emerging technologies in business processes.

2. Learning Outcomes: Upon successful completion of this course, the students will be able to (1) describe the roles of information systems; (2) discuss how information systems are used for competitive advantage; (3) explain how different information systems collect, retrieve, process, store, and disseminate information to the correct organizational units in a timely manner; (4) discuss how information systems are developed.

3. Required textbook: Essentials of Management Information Systems by Laudon & Laudon & Traver, 15th Edition, Pearson 2024

Examinations: The video proctored exams are of 50 multiple choice questions each, non-cumulative. They cover textbook materials as well as any additional materials provided. The exams must be completed within 50 minutes.

Case Analysis (CA): You will be conducting analyses on 2 selected cases from the textbook chapters. Your case analysis report should include a summary of each case (brief intro to the company or settings, challenges/problems, and solutions) and your answers to the case questions at the end of each case study. Your answers should be based on the chapter materials and any additional information found on the Internet. Clearly indicate the case question number. Limit your report to no more than 5 pages long, double spaced. Due date for each case analysis is indicated in D2L calendar / class schedule.

10. AI Use Policy
Student may use generative artificial intelligence (AI) and AI-assisted technologies in the writing and learning process. However, the AI tools are only used to improve readability or skills. Applying the technology should be done with human oversight and control, and students should carefully review and edit the result, as AI can generate authoritative-sounding output that can be incorrect, incomplete or biased. Any use of AI tools for completing the course assignments requires a declaration statement inserted at the top part of assignment submission: I USED AI TOOL, _______, FOR LEARNING ______________AND CHECKING ________. For assistance with writing style and documentation, please visit St. Cloud State University's writing center, The Write Place, http://www.stcloudstate.edu/writeplace/ or the Online writing Lab of Purdue University at http://owl.english.purdue.edu/
