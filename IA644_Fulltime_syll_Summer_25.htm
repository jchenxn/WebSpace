1. Course description: IA 644 is a full-time internship course designed for graduate students in the MSIA program who are engaged in an approved, full-time internship relevant to the field of Information Assurance or Cybersecurity. The course enables international students on an F-1 visa to remain in legal work status through Curricular Practical Training (CPT). Students must register for at least 1 credit of IA 644 during any semester in which they engage in full-time internship.                                   .

2. Learning Outcomes:  Please refer to a document titled IA644 Learning Outcomes in D2L course material content module.

3. Required Textbook: None  

4. Course Pre-requisites
(1). Approval of internship and CPT by the MSIA program and the Center for International Studies
(2). Good academic standing in the MSIA program   

5. Requirements 
There are no lectures or weekly assignments. Students must focus on their internship duties and submit a report when the internship is concluded, based on the following guidelines:

If you are graduating this semester: Submit a 15–20 page Internship Report by the final week of the semester . The report must follow the guidelines for internship paper located in the Course Content Module on D2L.
If you are continuing the internship beyond this semester: You do not need to submit the report this semester. Submit your report at the end of your internship in a future semester in which you are enrolled in IA 644.

6. Grading
A letter grade will be assigned based on your internship experience and paper quality.

7. Academic Integrity
All submissions must reflect your own professional experience and observations. Plagiarism or use of AI-generated content without proper disclosure is a violation of academic integrity policies.

 

8. Communication

 All official communication will be sent via D2L or university email. Check regularly for updates, reminders, and document templates.
