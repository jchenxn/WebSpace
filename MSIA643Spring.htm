1. Description: This course is about database security and auditing. It consists of three blocks: database security fundamentals, article readings, secuirty and auditing implementation in Oracle. Students will be exposed to both discretionary and mandatory security models. The course will focus on user administration, discretionary access control, virtual private database, and various auditing models. Other topics covered include SQL injection, statistical database security, data encryption. Students will learn how to implement database security on modern business databases using practical scenarios and step-by-step examples.

2. Objectives: Upon successfully completing this course, the students will have a solid understanding of database security and auditing models and techniques. Be able to implement database application security and auditing models.

3. Learning Outcomes: Be able to describe the security challenges and threats in database systems; Be able to demonstrate knowledge of basic information security architecture, database security models, and database auditing principles and methods; Be able to apply database access control methods; Be able to implement database security in business database applications; Be able to configure and conduct database auditing.

4. Required Textbook and Articles: Sam Afyouni, Database Security and Auditing: Protecting Data Integrity and Accessibility. Course Technology Incorporated, 2006, ISBN 0-619-21559-3. The articles are on the course D2L website.

Individual and Group Projects: You will complete six projects, of which some are individual projects. No late project/program is accepted unless you can provide official document of the extreme situation that prevented you from completing your project on time. No excuse is accepted after the project deadline.

Assigned Research Paper Presentations: You will be randomly assigned one paper to read and present in class. For each paper, you shall prepare approximately 30 minutes presentation on the paper and turn in a paper copy of your presentation before your presentation starts.

Group Term Paper Milestone Submissions: You will form a group of two persons to write a research paper on a topic assigned by the professor or of your choice with instructor's prior approval. The length of your paper should be in the range of 10 to 20 pages, double spaced. You will turn in four milestone drafts each representing a major section of your research paper, for example, Abstract/proposal, introduction, literature review, proposed solution/method, discussions and conclusions. Your final paper is due on the last class meet before the final exams week. Your submission should be a folder with all previously checked submissions inside! For how to write a research paper and examples of APA formatting, visit the course website.

Quizzes: Quizzes are of code writing or short answer type. They cover lectures and papers presented by students.

Exam: The exam questions are of short answer type and problem solving, which cover the lectures, projects, and papers.
