1. Course Objective: The course introduces students to the fundamentals of Management Information Systems and the relationship of information systems and business organizations. Students will learn the strategic and administrative roles of information systems in business, how information systems enable businesses to gain a competitive advantage, and the role of emerging technologies in business processes.

2. Learning Outcomes: Upon successful completion of this course, the studentsll be able to (1) describe the roles of information systems; (2) discuss how information systems are used for competitive advantage; (3) explain how different information systems collect, retrieve, process, store, and disseminate information to the correct organizational units in a timely manner; (4) discuss how information systems are developed.

3. Required textbook: Essentials of Management Information Systems by Laudon & Laudon, 12e Edition, Pearson 2017

Examinations: The exams are mutiple choice questions. The exams may cover material from book chapters even if that material is not covered in the lectures.

Pop Quizzes: Short quizzes are given at the beginning of class without or with prior announcement. They are of mutiple choice questions or/and short answer type.

Case Analysis and Presentation: You will be assigned to a team for a case analysis & presentation. You are required to read the assigned case and use Internet to find any current information about the case/company and any information system or technology used in the case. Your presentation should include an introduction to the company/case, and provide your analyses and answers to the case questions. Limit your presentation to 30 minutes plus 5 minutes Q & A. A paper copy of your presentation slides must be turned in before your presentation starts.

Projects: You will complete at least 4 individual projects and one group project. The project assignment sheets will be distributed to you at least one week before due date.

Class Participation: You are expected to attend all classes and participate in classroom discussions. Reading assignments for each class meeting are noted on the course syllabus. Class lectures will assume those assignments have been read. You will be called upon in class to answer questions. Each unexcused absence equals 1% reduction in class participation score (up to 5% max.). If you miss class 5 times, you would lose 5% of your total grade.
