Prerequisites

To enroll in this course, you should have successfully completed an introductory course in Management Information Systems such as BCIS 340 and a course in business statistics such as BCIS 542. It is expected that you have learned fundamentals of management information systems, basic knowledge in Microsoft Excel and Access.  In addition to the course prerequisites, students are expected to be able to read supplementary materials from some related textbooks and decision support system journal articles.

 

Course Description and Objectives

The general objective of this course is to provide students with knowledge of how to apply information technologies to support managerial decision making and improve the quality of decision making.  Topics include decision support systems model, decision modeling, SQL query for operational decision making, dataware housing and data mining, OLAP, group decision support systems (GDSS), executive information and support systems (EIS/ESS), expert systems (ES) and knowledge engineering.  The course focues on data warehousing and data mining for managerial decision support.

 

After the study of this course, it is expected that students will have the skills to effectively work with decision support systems and know conceptually how to apply information technology to support decision making and improve the quality of decision making.
