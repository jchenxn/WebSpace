1. Description: The development of efficient database applications requires an understanding of the fundamentals of database management systems, techniques for the design of databases and principles of database administration. This course introduces the DBMS concepts and database design techniques and principles. The emphasis is on both relational database conceptual design and implementation. Major topics include Entity Relationship Data Modeling, Structured Query Language, PL/SQL, database integrity, database security, concurrency control, Big Data, and NoSQL.

2. Objectives: Upon successfully completing this course, the students will understand the fundamentals of database system design and be familiar with the management of database systems and be able to develop database applications using Oracle, MySQL or other DBMS packages.

3. Learning Outcomes: (A) Develop entity-relationship models tailored for business applications. (B) Apply normalization techniques to optimize database performance in a business context. (C) Implement cloud-based database solutions to support business processes. (D) Utilize Structured Query Language (SQL) to enforce business rules and maintain data integrity. (E) Implement data security protocols to protect sensitive information. In addition, graduate students: (F) Be able to critically evaluate and propose advanced database security strategies, including encryption, access control, and auditing, to mitigate risks in modern database systems.

4. Required Textbook: Carlos Coronel and Steven Morris, Database Systems: Design, Implementation, and Management, 14th Edition, Cambridge, MA: Cengage Learning, 2023.

Individual Projects: You will complete three to five individual projects. The individual projects must be completed independently to receive full credits. No late program is accepted unless you can provide official document of the extreme situation that prevented you from completing your project on time. No excuse is accepted after the project deadline.

Group Projects: You will work in groups of four to develop a database system for a hypothetical business, whose descriptions will be given to you in the 5th week. You may form your own group if you attend the class in-person or request to be assigned to a group after you get the group project assignment sheet. Your group will make an in-person or online presentation at the end of the semester to demonstrate your system. The presentation is limited to about 20 minutes.Your individual group project score = group project score adjusted by a peer evaluation factor. Graduate students will be doing an extra assignment as part of GP#1. Unlike the first group project, the second group project on PL/SQL coding is very small and does not require presentation.

Exams & Quizzes: The examinations consist of multiple choice and short answer questions. Before taking exam 1 & 2, you may take on-line practice exams (see the course syllabus for links) on the same subject matters as the actual exam. The scores of the practice exams are not recorded and are not part of your course grade. There will be two to four quizzes. The quizzes are multiple choice, problem solving or short answer type questions. No makeup test or quiz is allowed.

Class Participation: Participation is an important part of your learning in this Flexible delivery course. Because students may attend in person or study asynchronously, participation is assessed in the following ways:

  • Studying Course Materials: D2L tracks your time and progress on course materials, including recorded lecture videos and weekly reading assignments. Your participation score is based primarily on your engagement with these materials.

  • In-person Attendance: If you attend the class in person, your attendance at the designated class times will also be factored into your participation score.

  • Classroom Discussions: Whether in person or online, you are expected to come prepared by watching the lecture videos and completing the assigned readings. In class, you may be called upon to answer questions or contribute to discussions.

Participation is evaluated on your consistent effort to stay engaged with the course, not on the quantity of contributions.

You must understand the logic behind any code you submit for grading and be able to solve similar problems without using AI tools on the exams and quizzes.
Any use of AI tools for completing the course assignments requires a declaration statement inserted at the top part of assignment submission: I USED AI TOOL, _______, FOR LEARNING ______________AND CHECKING ________.
