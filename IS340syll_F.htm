DESCRIPTION This is an introduction to Management Information Systems course. The primary objective is to introduce students to the fundamentals of the relationship of information systems and business organizations. Students will learn the strategic and administrative roles of information systems in business, describe how information systems enable businesses to gain a competitive advantage, and anticipate the role of emerging technologies on business processes.
Course objectives: (1). Describe the roles of Information Systems (2). Discuss how Information Systems are used for competitive advantage. (3). Explain how different information systems collect, retrieve, process, store, and disseminate information to the correct organizational units in a timely manner. (4). Discuss how information systems are developed.

Exminations: The exams are mutiple choice questions. You may do practice tests/quizzes from the publisher for each chapter before the exams. The exams may cover material from book chapters even if that material is not covered in the lectures.

Case Analysis and Presentation: You will be assigned to a team for a case analysis & presentation. You are required to read the assigned case in the chapter. Then, use Internet to find any updated information about the case/company and any information system or technology used in the case. Your presentation should include an introduction to the company/case, and provide your analyses and answers to the case questions. Limit your presentation to 30 minutes plus 5 minutes Q & A. A paper copy of your presentation slides must be turned in before your presentation starts.

Projects: You will complete four team projects. The project assignment sheets will be distributed to you at least one week before due date. Web design projects must be presented to the class in the last week of classes before the final exam.

Short Writing Assignments: You will be given a number of short writing assignments throughtout the semester. The assignments are of 1 to 2 page short paper. Most papers may require you to google search for information or library search to supplement your textbook in order to complete your assignments. Your paper should have a title, section headings, and references at the end.You will be asked to talk about your findings in class.

Class Participation:You are expected to attend all classes and participate in class discussions. Reading assignments for each class meeting are noted on the course syllabus. Class lectures will assume those assignments have been read. You will be called upon in class to answer questions. Each unexcused absence equals 1% reduction in class participation score. If you miss class 5 times, you would lose 5% of your total grade.

Course Grades:Your grade in this course will be determined by your individual and group performance. I typically use the following scale to assign letter grades: 91-100% = A, 81-90% = B, 71-80% = C, 61-70% = D, < 61% = F. I reserve the right to adjust this scale.
