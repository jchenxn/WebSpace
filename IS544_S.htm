DESCRIPTION This course is designed to provide pre-MBA students foundation knowledge in information technology. Students will learn the strategic and administrative roles of information systems in business, describe how information systems enable businesses to gain a competitive advantage, and anticipate the role of emerging technologies on business processes.
Course objectives: (1). Describe the roles of Information Systems (2). Discuss how Information Systems are used for competitive advantage. (3). Explain how different information systems collect, retrieve, process, store, and disseminate information to the correct organizational units in a timely manner. (4). Discuss how information systems are developed.

Examinations: The exams are mutiple choice questions from M: Information Systems and short answer questions from the HBR case studies presented & discussed in class. You may do practice tests/quizzes for each chapter before the exams.

Projects: You will complete two projects. The project assignment sheets will be distributed to you at least one week before due date.

HBR Case Study Summaries: You will write at least 2 page case summary for each of the selected cases except the one you are assigned to present. Your summary should include important facts/events, the essence of the case study, lessons learned, your insights and suggestions, etc. Your summary should also include at least two well-thought questions for classroom discussions. Organize your summary into sections with meaningful section headings. You will be asked to talk about the cases. Follow the link on this course's web page to buy the Harvard Business Review case studies.

Case Discussion Leader: You will be assigned a case to be its co-discussion leader. Your professor will assist you in the class discussions. You may choose to do a presentation (30+ minutes) on the case background, then followed by case discussion questions. Your case questions should stir debate or discussions that help the class learn. A paper copy of your presentation slides and discussion questions must be turned in via email at leat 1 hour before your presentation starts.

Class Participation: You are expected to attend all classes and participate in class discussions. Chapter & case study reading assignments for each week are noted on the course syllabus. Class lectures will assume those readings have been done. You will be called upon in class to answer questions. Your participation score is determine by both your participation frequency and quality.

Course Grades:Your course grade will be determined by your individual performance. I typically use the following scale to assign letter grades: 91-100% = A, 81-90% = B, 71-80% = C, 61-70% = D, < 61% = F. I reserve the right to adjust this scale.
