1. Description: The development of efficient database applications requires an understanding of the fundamentals of database management systems, techniques for the design of databases and principles of database administration. This course introduces the DBMS concepts and database design techniques and principles. The emphasis is on both relational database conceptual design and implementation. Major topics include Entity Relationship Data Modeling, Structured Query Language, PL/SQL, database integrity, database security, concurrency control, Big Data, and NoSQL.

2. Objectives: Upon successfully completing this course, the students will understand the fundamentals of database system design and be familiar with the management of database systems and be able to develop database applications using Oracle, MySQL or other DBMS packages.

3. Learning Outcomes: Be able to explain database concepts and processes; Be able to conduct conceptual database design, normalization, and physical implementation; Be proficient in writing SQL code; Be proficient in writing PL/SQL code; Be able to use triggers to enforce business rules; Be able to demonstrate knowledge in transaction management.

4. Required textbook:Carlos Coronel and Steven Morris, Database Systems: Design, Implementation, and Management, 13th Edition, Cambridge, MA: Cengage Learning, 2019.

Individual Projects: You will complete three to five individual projects. The individual projects must be completed independently to receive full credits. No late program is accepted unless you can provide official document of the extreme situation that prevented you from completing your project on time. No excuse is accepted after the project deadline.

Group Project: You will work in groups of four to develop a database system for a hypothetical business, whose descriptions will be given to you in the 5th week. You may form your own group or request to be assigned to a group after you get the group project assignment sheet. Your group will make an in-class presentation at the end of the semester to demonstrate your system. The presentation is limited to about 20 minutes.Your individual group project score = group project score adjusted by a peer evaluation factor.

Exams & Quizzes: The examinations consist of multiple choice and short answer questions. Before taking exam 1 & 2, you may take on-line practice exams (see my website for links) on the same subject matters as the actual exam. The scores of the practice exams are not recorded and are not part of your course grade. There will be three to five quizzes. The quizzes are problem solving or short answer type questions. No makeup test or quiz is allowed.

Class Participation: You are expected to attend all classes and participate in classroom discussions. You are encouraged to turn on video during zoom meetings.
